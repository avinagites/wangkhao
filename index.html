Brief Profile

Wangkhao Government College is the only institute of higher education in the entire district of Mon, Nagaland. Nagaland lies in the north east of India.

The College was established in July 5, 1983 as a private college, but took off as community institute for the entire Konyak tribe. Literacy rate of the Konyak community at the time was less than 30 per cent. Gradually some enlightened students gained an inkling of what it meant to have a college for the people of the district.

The humble beginning of the college saw a steady growth within a few years. The college is now nestled at a scenic hill top, away from the hustles of the town occupying 65 acres of land and among the largest colleges in Nagaland in terms of land area.

Community initiative

Public interest was roused and spontaneous response to the call for the noble cause thus came by way of each household contributing a token donation of Rs. 2 (Rupees two) as seed money to start the college.

Government Recognised

The college was taken over by the State Government in the year 1990. It is affiliated under Nagaland University (NU), a Central University. The college is also affiliated with the University Grants Commission (UGC) under Section 12(b) and (f).

Subject Discipline

The college offers Arts discipline up to undergraduate course; Science stream is in the process of being inducted.

Undergraduate arts stream

  1. English: Compulsory
  2. Economics: Optional
  3. Education: Optional
  4. History: Optional
  5. Political Science : Optional
  6. Sociology: Optional
